Understanding Your Legal Rights in Ohio

Knowing your rights under Ohio law is the first step in protecting yourself and your family. Here are key areas where you may need legal assistance:

1. Family Law

Parental Rights:

  • Married Parents: Both mothers and fathers have equal rights to legal and residential custody of their children when married at the time of birth
  • Unmarried Parents: The mother typically has sole custody until a court order designates otherwise

Child Support:

  • Ohio uses an Income Shares Model that considers both parents' incomes
  • Designed to ensure children receive the same financial support as if parents lived together

2. Housing Rights

Landlord Obligations:

  • Provide a habitable and safe living environment
  • Comply with housing codes
  • Provide necessary services (water, heat, trash removal)
  • Give at least 24-hour notice before entering (except emergencies)

Tenant Rights:

  • Right to a safe and habitable dwelling
  • Right to complain about violations without retaliation
  • Right to join with other tenants
  • Protection from improper eviction procedures

üè† Facing Eviction?

Landlords must follow specific legal procedures for eviction. Contact legal aid immediately if you receive an eviction notice to understand your rights and options.

3. Consumer Protection

Consumer Sales Practices Act:

  • Protects against unfair, deceptive, or unconscionable practices
  • Ensures businesses engage in fair and honest practices
  • Provides remedies for consumer fraud

Vehicle Purchase Protection:

  • Right to cancel vehicle purchase if title isn't delivered within required timeframe
  • Protection under the Certificate of Motor Vehicle Title Act

4. Labor & Employment Law

Minimum Wage (as of January 1, 2025):

  • Non-tipped employees: $10.70 per hour
  • Tipped employees: $5.35 per hour

Workplace Rights:

  • Protection from discrimination based on protected characteristics
  • Right to file wage complaints with Ohio Department of Commerce
  • Access to Ohio Civil Rights Commission for discrimination issues

How to Access Legal Aid in Ohio

Legal aid organizations provide free legal help to qualifying individuals and families. Here's how to access these services:

Finding Legal Aid

  • Start with your local Legal Aid Society - they can assess your eligibility and connect you with appropriate services
  • Use online directories to find the legal aid office serving your area
  • Contact Advocates for Basic Legal Equality (ABLE) for additional services

Look for the mobile legal aid office in your community! The Ohio Justice Bus brings free legal services and technology access directly to neighborhoods.

What Legal Aid Can Help With

  • Housing issues (evictions, landlord problems, housing discrimination)
  • Family law matters (custody, divorce, domestic violence)
  • Public benefits applications and appeals
  • Consumer protection issues
  • Employment problems and wage theft
  • Healthcare access and insurance issues
